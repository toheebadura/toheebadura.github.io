About Me

Personal Profile

I am currently a PhD student in Data Science with the Science Foundation Ireland Centre for Research Training in Foundations of Data Science. I am working on the applications of natural language processing in the context of low-resource African languages.

I previously earned a National Diploma in Mathematics and Statistics from the Moshood Abiola Polytechnic, Abeokuta, Ogun State, Nigeria. I also hold a Bachelor of Science in Statistics from the University of Ibadan, Nigeria. Subsequently, I was awarded a fully-funded Master's scholarship in Mathematical Science through the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences, Rwanda.

I am interested in the applications of machine learning and data science in solving real-world problems. I am passionate about teaching and mentoring students in the field and have experience in teaching mathematics, statistics and computer science to high school, undergraduate and postgraduate level students.

I am always looking for opportunities to collaborate on research projects to promote language diversity and improve the representation of African languages in technology. Feel free to reach out if you have any questions or would like to collaborate.
